# Voice and Tone

How Winningtemp talks — the same identity expressed in English, Swedish, and Norwegian.

This file is the lens for **all** customer-facing copy: ads, landing pages, blog posts, decks, sales emails, in-product text, social, support replies, internal comms intended for external eyes.

The tone is shaped by the **four brand values** — read `references/brand-values.md` first.

## Voice attributes

Four words. Memorize them.

1. **Honest** — claims are specific and defensible. We acknowledge limits.
2. **Modern** — current language, no jargon, no old corporate constructions.
3. **Warm** — talking like a colleague who cares about the work, not a vendor pitching.
4. **Inclusive** — the audience is the entire workforce, not just executives.

These four are non-negotiable. Tone (formal/casual, urgent/calm) flexes with context. Voice does not.

## Tone calibration

Tone is **how** we say it; voice is **what we sound like**. Adjust tone for the situation:

| Context | Tone shifts |
|---|---|
| Sales deck for a CHRO | Slightly more measured. Lead with business outcomes. Cite data. |
| Sales deck for a frontline manager | More direct, more human. Show what their day looks like with the product. |
| Ad creative (top-of-funnel) | Hook-first, conversational, single-point-per-asset |
| Educational guide / report | Authoritative but not academic — friendly expertise |
| Customer email | Warmer, briefer, written like a person sent it |
| Internal comms (about us) | Energetic, candid, occasional humor |
| Crisis / outage comms | Direct, factual, calm. No hedge words. |
| Legal / compliance copy | Plain language. Still avoid legalese where possible. |

Voice stays the same in all of these.

## Words we use / words we don't

### ✅ Use these

**Verbs:** ask, listen, notice, learn, build, share, work, deliver, change, support, lead, follow up, measure, improve, uncover.

**Nouns:** team, manager, employee, feedback, signal, conversation, engagement, energy, work, day-to-day, the workforce, frontline, leader, partnership, culture, momentum.

**Adjectives:** honest, real, clear, useful, practical, specific, measured, human, modern, fair, calm.

**Connectors:** because, so that, which is why, here's how.

### ❌ Avoid these

**Buzzwords:** synergy, leverage, robust, scalable (in marketing — fine in engineering), best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge, paradigm, holistic, frictionless, seamless (overused), turnkey.

**Hype:** revolutionary, groundbreaking, game-changing, transformative, disruptive, unparalleled, next-generation, unlock potential, supercharge.

**Hedges:** up to X%, as much as, could lead to, may help, might, hopefully.

**Vague claims:** improve performance, drive results, deliver value, increase ROI (without specifics).

**Adjectives that drain energy:** powerful, robust, comprehensive, end-to-end, all-in-one.

**Person-coded jargon:** "rockstar," "ninja," "guru" — for any role.

**Worn metaphors:** moving the needle, low-hanging fruit, boil the ocean, deep dive, circle back, take it offline.

## Sentence-level rules

1. **Active voice.** "Managers see weekly trends" beats "Weekly trends are visible to managers."
2. **Short sentences.** If a sentence has more than two commas, break it.
3. **Concrete over abstract.** "85% of teams notice a drop within a week" beats "Improved team awareness."
4. **One idea per sentence.** Two ideas = two sentences.
5. **Specific verbs.** "Cuts manager check-in time by 40%" beats "Helps with check-ins."
6. **No "We are pleased to announce."** Just announce it.
7. **Don't open with the company name** in marketing copy. Open with the reader.
8. **Numbers are powerful — but only with sources.** Always attribute (footnote, link, "according to...").

## Headlines and CTAs

### Headline patterns that work
- **Question hooks:** "Why did engagement drop in Q2?" — leans into Curiosity.
- **Specific outcome:** "How a 600-person manufacturer cut absenteeism in 6 months."
- **Direct provocation:** "Annual surveys are dying. Here's what's replacing them."
- **Empathic problem:** "When the engagement score moves, your manager should know first."

### Headline patterns to avoid
- "Empower your workforce" — meaningless verb.
- "Unleash the potential of your team" — meaningless metaphor.
- "Transform your culture today" — empty hype.
- "The world's leading engagement platform" — unverifiable boast.

### CTA patterns
✅ Use: "Book a demo," "See it live," "Get the report," "Try Winningtemp," "Talk to us," "Read the case study," "Start free."
❌ Avoid: "Learn more" (lazy), "Click here" (anti-pattern), "Submit" (cold), "Get started today!" (the exclamation is doing too much work).

## Multilingual notes

Winningtemp operates across the Nordics, primarily in **Swedish (SE)** and **Norwegian (NO)**, plus **English (EN)** for international and B2B sales. Treat these as first-class languages, not translations.

### Swedish (SE)

- **Tone:** Slightly warmer than English. Direct but never abrupt. The "lagom" instinct applies — confident, not loud.
- **Forms of address:** Use **du** (informal you), never **Ni** (formal), even with executives. Sweden moved past Ni decades ago in business contexts.
- **Avoid Anglicisms:** prefer Swedish equivalents when one exists. "Engagemang" not "engagement," "ledarskap" not "leadership," "feedback" is acceptable (loanword in common use).
- **Punctuation/grammar:** lowercase day/month names, decimal *comma* (10,5 not 10.5), thousands separator is space (10 000 not 10,000).
- **Don't translate idioms literally** from English. Rewrite for the meaning.
- **Common honest words:** ärlig, tydlig, rak, vänlig, mänsklig, modern, praktisk.

### Norwegian (NO)

- **Variant:** Default to **Bokmål** for written communications (this is what the Visual identity slides demonstrate). Confirm dialect requirement for any specific customer.
- **Tone:** Similar to Swedish in directness, but Norwegian tends to be slightly more informal. Use **du** as default.
- **Cognates with Swedish:** many words are very close (engasjement / engagemang, ledelse / ledarskap). Don't just transliterate — use the Norwegian form.
- **Punctuation/grammar:** lowercase day/month names; decimal comma; thousands separator is space.
- **Æ / Ø / Å:** use the correct letters (these aren't optional).

### English (EN)

- **Default to British English** for European audiences (organise, behaviour, colour).
- **Default to American English** only when the target audience is explicitly North American.
- Don't mix within a single piece.

### Translation workflow
- Never auto-translate ad creative or hero copy. Have a native speaker re-write for tone.
- Direct word-for-word translation almost always produces awkward phrasing in SE/NO.
- The same insight should land emotionally the same in all three languages, not just literally the same.

## Audience-specific tweaks

| Audience | What to lead with | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **CHRO / People leader** | Strategic + measurable outcomes. Org-wide impact. Trend data. | Tactical "how it works" details — they have teams for that |
| **HR Business Partner** | Practical workflows, time savings, fewer one-off requests | Vague C-suite framing |
| **Line manager** | "What does my Monday look like?" Concrete, day-to-day examples | Strategic / org-wide framing they can't influence |
| **C-suite (CEO/CFO)** | ROI, retention impact, productivity, cost-of-disengagement | Product features. Translate to outcomes. |
| **Employee end-user** | Trust, privacy, "your voice matters" — the contract with the platform | Marketing language; speak as a real human comms |
| **IT / procurement** | Security, integrations, deployment, compliance (GDPR, ISO) | Brand storytelling; they want facts |

## Example rewrites

### Before (off-brand)
> Winningtemp is a powerful, all-in-one engagement platform that empowers teams to unlock their full potential and drive transformative business outcomes.

### After (on-brand)
> Winningtemp gives managers a weekly read on how their team is actually doing — so they can act before small problems become big ones.

---

### Before (off-brand SE)
> Winningtemp är en kraftfull plattform som hjälper er att maximera era teams potential och driva transformerande resultat.

### After (on-brand SE)
> Winningtemp ger chefer en tydlig bild av hur teamet mår — varje vecka. Så att ni kan agera medan det fortfarande är enkelt.

### Before (lazy CTA)
> Click here to learn more about how our solutions can help you.

> See how it works → (1-minute product tour)

## Final checklist

Before sending any branded copy to publish:

```
- [ ] Voice = honest, modern, warm, inclusive
- [ ] At least one of the 4 brand values shows up naturally
- [ ] No buzzwords, hype words, or worn metaphors from the lists above
- [ ] Active voice, short sentences, concrete examples
- [ ] Numbers cited with sources
- [ ] CTA is specific (not "Learn more")
- [ ] Audience-appropriate framing
- [ ] If multilingual: native review, not auto-translate
- [ ] Reads like a person who cares wrote it
```

## Common mistakes

1. **Defaulting to "powerful, scalable, robust"** when describing the product.
2. **"Up to X%" claims** — too easy to dismiss. Use a specific number with a source.
3. **Translating English ad copy directly into Swedish/Norwegian** — creates stilted prose.
4. **Using "Ni" (formal you) in Swedish** — sounds outdated. Use "du."
5. **Headlines that name the company first.** Lead with the reader's problem.
6. **CTAs that say "Learn more."** Be specific about what the user gets.
7. **"We are excited to announce"** — just announce it.
8. **Writing for the C-suite when the actual reader is a manager** (or vice versa).
9. **Adding emoji to formal/sales copy** because they're "warm." Stay text-led.
10. **Citing competitor brands by name** unless brand-team approved.
